OUR STORY

What is now King Eddy Saloon was born in 1906 as a watering hole for the then high-class King Edward Hotel on E 5th Street. During Prohibition, King Eddy opened a speakeasy in its basement while operating its upstairs as a piano store front. Repeal Day 1933 was its official christening as the King Eddy. And the bar maintained success until L.A.’s Red Car electric railway system died out. But King Eddy continued to serve through it all, from a decades-long neighborhood drug epidemic, the likes of Bukowski, to today's invasion of mixologists. It’s the last of its kind near Skid Row.
